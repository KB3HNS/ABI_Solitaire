Four Corners - Game Rules
General:
    Four Corners is played using 52 cards - Ace through King in four suits.  The Ace is both the high and low card.

The Board:
    The board is divided up into 3 sections:
       1:  The main board - Cards are placed in 5 columbs in descending value.

       2:  The four corners - cards are moved here in suit and ascending sequence, the upper left corner contains the starting card.

       3:  The draw / discard pile.  The draw pile initially contains the remaining 46 cards.

The Main Board:
       The main board consists of three rows and three columbs like a tic-tac-toe board.  The outer four corners are effectively the goal section of the game.  The five center positions are dealt one card each and the upper - left hand corner is dealt a card.  The last carrd dealt is the start card.  Each of the four corners are started with the card of the same face value as the start carrd, but more on this later.  The remainder of this section covers the rules for the five central columbs.  Cards can be moved to this section in descending order.  Only the face value of the card is important in this section, the suit or color does not matter.  I.E.  Any 9 may be played on a 10 of spades.  There is no limit as to how deep each columb gets.

The Four Corners of the Main Board:
       The objective of the game is to move all of the cards to the four corners.  This is done by starting with the cards with the same value as the start card and moving the cards to the corners in increasing value by suit.

The Draw / Discard Pile:
       Cards are turned over one at a time from the draw pile.  The cards can be moved to the main board or the discard pile.  The top card in the discard pile can still be played, revealing the next card.
