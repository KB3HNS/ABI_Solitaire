Pyrimid - Game Rules
General:
    Pyrimid is played using 52 cards - Ace through King in four suits.  The Ace is the low card and the King is the high card. 

The Board:
    The board is divided up into 2 sections:
       1:  The main board - Cards are dealt here in a pyrimid fashion in 7 rows.  1 card in the first, centered.  2 in the second, etc.  All the way to row 7 which contains 7 cards for a total of 28.

       2:  The draw / discard pile.  The draw pile initially contains the remaining 24 cards.

The Main Board:
       The cards in the main board can be selected and played only on the following criteria.
          1.  There are no cards directly below the card.
          2.  You are trying to match a card with the only card below it. 

The Draw / Discard Pile:
       Cards are turned over one at a time from the draw pile.  They can either be played or moved to the discard pile which is directly to the right.  If a card is moved to the discard pile, not all is lost.  The top discarded card may be played along with the top card in the draw pile.  Those cards can also be matched and played.  Any card that meets the selection criteria can be used to match.

The Goal:
       The objective of pyrimid is to eliminate all of the cards.  Every time one or two cards are matched, they are eliminated from the game.  When all cards have been eliminated, from the draw pile, discard pile, and pyrimid, the game is won.  Cards in the discard pile can not be moved to the main board. 


Good Luck!  And, Enjoy! :-)
